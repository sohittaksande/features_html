Energy Redistribution:

The system monitors real-time solar energy production. If excess solar energy is generated beyond household consumption needs, it can be stored in batteries or redirected back to the grid. By selling surplus energy to the grid during peak tariff times, users can earn credits or savings, thus reducing overall energy costs.

Energy Savings and Storage Management:

The system tracks energy production and consumption patterns and stores surplus solar energy in battery storage. During high-tariff periods, the system prioritizes using stored solar energy instead of grid energy, reducing reliance on the grid and optimizing energy savings. Any remaining stored energy can be sold to the grid.

Data Analytics for Energy Optimization:

Machine learning models analyze energy usage patterns, solar production, and grid tariffs in real time. By leveraging historical and real-time data, the system predicts future consumption trends, identifies optimal times for energy redistribution, and suggests the best times to sell energy back to the grid.
